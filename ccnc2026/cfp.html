Call for Papers

6G-SAGA: Intelligent Aerial and Spaceborne Systems for 6G – Communication, Sensing, and Autonomy

In conjunction with IEEE CCNC 2026

Workshop Scope

The 6G-SAGA workshop invites original research contributions and visionary papers on the integration of intelligent aerial and spaceborne systems into 6G networks. The workshop aims to explore the evolution of Satellite–Aerial–Ground Integrated Networks (SAGIN) and beyond, focusing on AI-native architectures, joint communication and sensing, and autonomous operations.

Topics of Interest

  • AI-native architectures for SAGIN and Beyond-SAGIN systems
  • Joint communication, computing, and sensing (3CS) in aerial/spaceborne networks
  • Edge intelligence and federated learning in non-terrestrial environments
  • UAV/HAPS-assisted dynamic network slicing and orchestration
  • Satellite-terrestrial interoperability and multi-orbit coordination
  • Spectrum sharing and interference mitigation in NTNs
  • AI for autonomous mission planning and aerial swarm systems
  • Security and privacy in multi-layered aerial and space networks
  • Experimental testbeds and real-world deployments for aerial/space 6G

Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished research papers (maximum 6 pages) in IEEE conference format. All submissions will be peer-reviewed, and accepted papers will be published in the IEEE CCNC 2026 Conference Proceedings and submitted to IEEE Xplore.

Submission details and deadlines will be available on the workshop website: http://www.6gsaga-workshop.org
