Biographies

Prof. Shigeru Shimamoto (Waseda University, Japan)

Prof. Shimamoto is a Professor in the Department of Communications and Computer Engineering at Waseda University, Japan. His research focuses on satellite and mobile communications, advanced access schemes, and UAV-assisted disaster communication networks. He has extensive experience organizing major IEEE conferences and workshops.

Prof. Trung Q. Duong (Memorial University of Newfoundland, Canada)

Prof. Duong is a Canada Excellence Research Chair and Full Professor at Memorial University. His research spans wireless communications, 6G networks, quantum machine learning, and non-terrestrial networks. He is Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials and an IEEE Distinguished Lecturer.

Prof. Haejoon Jung (Kyung Hee University, South Korea)

Prof. Jung specializes in non-terrestrial networks and intelligent communication. He has served as an editor for multiple IEEE journals and has received awards for his contributions to wireless systems.

Assoc. Prof. Zhenni Pan (Waseda University, Japan)

Dr. Pan focuses on green wireless communications, mobile network systems, and air-to-ground communications. She serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE Internet of Things Journal and has contributed to major IEEE conferences.

Assoc. Prof. Tu Dac Ho (NTNU, Norway)

Dr. Ho’s research centers on integrating machine learning and AI into beyond-5G/6G networks, with emphasis on UAV-assisted communications and sustainable IoT. He has organized several international workshops and serves as Associate Editor for IEEE IoT Journal.
